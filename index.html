Forest

"a large area of land covered with trees and plants, usually larger than a wood, or the trees and plants themselves"(Cambridge Dictionary).

Facts

Trees are connected by mycorrhizal fungi networks, often called the wood wide web. Through this network, trees can share nutrients and even send “warnings” when pests are present.

Tropical rainforests cover only about 6-7% of the Earth's surface, yet they are home to more than half of all terrestrial species.

Dense vegetation absorbs and deflects sound, which is why forested areas are often significantly quieter than urban environments.

A single large tree can release hundreds of liters of water into the atmosphere each day.

“Forests are the lungs of the earth, purifying the air and giving fresh strength to our people.”

-Wangari Maathai, Nobel Peace Prize laureate
